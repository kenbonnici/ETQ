Horizon.

A rising sun meeting the line — the horizon you earned.

The Horizon mark is the primary identity for ETQ (Enough to Quit). This page documents its anatomy, palette, sizing, contexts, and rules of use, so that any future surface — landing page, app, favicon, print, social — renders the mark the same way.

The mark

primary · full-colour

A horizon, not a chart.

Two arcs — a warm amber half-sun over a softer inner glow — meeting a steady forest-green horizon line. The sun is just rising, not setting; the line is a threshold, not a limit.

What it says

Freedom. A beginning. Light arriving, not fading.

What it is not

A bar chart, a ticker, a badge, or a checkmark of completion.

Anatomy

construction · 40 × 40 grid

Outer sun · amber warm

Half-disc, radius 12u, centred on the horizon. The warmest body in the mark; carries emotional weight.

Inner glow · amber highlight

Concentric half-disc, radius 7u, opacity 0.6. Suggests depth and dawn light spilling from the core.

Horizon line · forest

Stroke 1.8u, rounded caps, from x=4 to x=36. The threshold. Never broken, never tilted.

Scale

Below 16 px the inner glow ceases to add information and the horizon line becomes visually noisy. Use the Full Stop variant (amber-filled forest ring) for favicons or touch icons at or below this size.

Clear space

minimum breathing room · x = horizon extension

Rule: maintain clear space equal to x — the distance from the end of the horizon line to the edge of the viewBox — on all four sides of the mark.

At 40 px body size, that's roughly 4 px of breathing room. Do not crop. Do not nest inside tight UI.

When locked up with the wordmark, the space between the mark and the "E" of "Enough" should be approximately 0.4x.

Why a different mark here.

At 16 px the Horizon mark's inner glow washes out and the horizon line visually merges with browser chrome. The Full Stop — a forest ring around an amber core — reads cleanly at that size and acts as the system favicon and app icon.

Everywhere else (24 px and up), use the Horizon mark.

Do & don't

rules of use

Do
  • Keep the horizon line perfectly horizontal. The line is the steadiness of the promise.
  • Preserve clear space of x on all sides.
  • Use the Full Stop variant at or below 16 px.
  • Pair with Cormorant Garamond (display) or Inter (UI) wordmark — never another face.
  • Use the dark-bg variant on forest, dusk, or hero imagery where contrast demands it.
Don't
  • Rotate, flip, or skew. A rising sun is not a setting one.
  • Re-colour the sun cool (blue, purple, grey). The warmth is the point.
  • Stretch non-uniformly or change the 40 × 40 proportions.
  • Place on low-contrast or busy photography without a scrim.
  • Add taglines, drop shadows, glows, or container boxes.

drop-in source · cream / paper background

  <path d="M 8 26 A 12 12 0 0 1 32 26" fill="#c98a3f"/>
  <!-- inner glow -->
  <path d="M 13 26 A 7 7 0 0 1 27 26" fill="#e6a45a" opacity="0.6"/>

  <line x1="4" y1="26" x2="36" y2="26"
        stroke="#1f3a35" stroke-width="1.8" stroke-linecap="round"/>
</svg>

For dark backgrounds: change the inner glow to #f3d9a4 and the horizon line to #f8f2e6. Leave the outer sun as #c98a3f — it holds its warmth against both light and dark fields.

Wordmark lockups: when the mark sits beside the wordmark, use the tight viewBox "4 12 32 16" (aspect 2:1) so the mark visually matches the cap-height of the text. The full square "0 0 40 40" viewBox is for standalone uses — app icon tiles, print plates, the spec itself.
