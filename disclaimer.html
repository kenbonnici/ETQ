Assumptions and limitations

ETQ is a single-path deterministic model. It does not run probability simulations and does not produce confidence intervals. Small changes in assumptions can produce materially different results.

Default assumptions used by the model:

  • General inflation: 2.0% per year
  • Cash interest rate: 2.0% per year
  • Equity return: 6.5% per year (long-run nominal estimate)
  • Property appreciation: 3.0% per year
  • Salary growth: 3.0% per year
  • Rental income growth: 2.0% per year
  • Stock selling cost: 5.0% of proceeds
  • Property disposal cost: 15.0% of proceeds
  • Other-asset disposal cost: 5.0% of proceeds
  • Default life expectancy: 90; default early-pension reduction: 500 / year of early retirement

ETQ does not model income tax, capital gains tax, social security or national insurance contributions, jurisdiction-specific pension or tax wrappers, healthcare or long-term care costs, currency risk on multi-currency portfolios, sequence-of-returns risk, or regulatory or legislative change. Income and pension figures should be entered net of tax.

Detailed methodology, assumption sensitivity, and the full risk warnings for investment, tax, pension, inflation, property, and market risk are being prepared.

Privacy

ETQ runs entirely in your browser. Your inputs are not transmitted to any server operated by ETQ. To let you return to your work, ETQ stores your inputs and saved scenarios in your browser's local storage and session storage on this device, under keys beginning with etq:. This storage is not encrypted and is accessible to anyone using the same browser profile on this device.

Clearing your browser data, switching browser, switching device, or using private or incognito mode will lose any data ETQ has stored. There is no cloud backup and no recovery process.

ETQ does not use cookies, analytics, or third-party trackers. The site currently loads web fonts from Google Fonts, which transmits your IP address to Google solely so the fonts can be delivered. ETQ does not receive that data. (Self-hosting these fonts is on the roadmap.)
