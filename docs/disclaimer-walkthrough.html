Operator visual reference

ETQ — disclaimer package

Every visual component the user encounters, in the order they encounter it. With trigger conditions and instructions to reproduce.

Last updated: 2026-05-07 · For: Kenneth Bonnici (operator)

How to use this guide

This document walks you, as a user, through every disclosure currently in ETQ. It is structured chronologically — the first surface a user lands on, then onboarding, then the full calculator, then the disclaimer page itself. For each component you'll see what you see, when it appears, and how to trigger it from a clean browser.

Each component card uses the same anatomy: copy is shown verbatim in the dashed box; the orange labels mark the operational metadata. The cards in cream are the dismissible ones — once acknowledged, they go away (until storage is cleared).

To start with a clean state at any point, jump to the Reset cheatsheet on the last page.

1

Landing page (first arrival)

URL: http://127.0.0.1:4173/ETQ/

Always-visible disclosures the user encounters before clicking anything. None require dismissal.

1.1 Trust-row pip

What you see A horizontal row of five pips beneath the hero CTA buttons. The fifth one reads Not financial advice.
When Always visible while the user is on the landing page above the fold.
How to trigger Open the landing URL. The trust row is immediately visible under the "See if you can quit" button.

1.2 Quick-estimate privacy line

What you see A small italic line at the top of the six-input calculator card.
Verbatim copy
All in your browser. Nothing leaves your device. Your inputs are remembered locally so you can come back to them; clear them any time.
When Always visible.
How to trigger Scroll down to the "Thirty-second estimate" section on the landing page.

1.3 Quick-estimate result label and inline pip

What you see An eyebrow label above the big age number, plus a subdued line beneath it. The label is case-aware:
  • Empty / no result: Earliest viable at + em-dash placeholder
  • Future case (age > current): Earliest viable at age + the age
  • "Now" case (age = current): Viable now, at age + the age
Verbatim pip
Illustrative model output, not financial advice. Sensitive to your inputs and ETQ's default assumptions. See limitations.
When Label always visible. Pip always visible.
How to trigger To see the case differentiation: in the calculator card, type your age in the first field, then enter inputs that produce different earliest-viable ages. Try age 45 with very low expenses and high savings to see "Viable now, at age 45". Try age 45 with normal expenses to see "Earliest viable at age 56" (or similar).

1.4 Bio non-MFSA/FCA-authorisation line

What you see A small subdued italic-style paragraph at the bottom of the credibility section, separated from the bio by a thin top border.
Verbatim
ETQ is not authorised by the MFSA or the FCA, and does not provide regulated financial advice or a personal recommendation.
When Always visible.
How to trigger Scroll past the calculator card on the landing to the "Not another 4% rule blog" section. The line sits below the four bio paragraphs.

1.5 Footer slogan and link strip

What you see A short statement on the left of the page footer plus three right-aligned links: Disclaimer · Privacy · About.
Verbatim slogan
© ETQ · Enough to Quit. A deterministic retirement-projection tool. For educational and informational use only. Not financial, tax, legal, or pension advice.
When Always visible at the bottom of the landing page.
How to trigger Scroll to the very bottom. The links lead to disclaimer.html#disclaimer, #privacy, #about.
2

Onboarding walkthrough

URL: http://127.0.0.1:4173/ETQ/onboarding.html

One-time and persistent disclosures encountered during the guided question flow.

2.1 First-visit acknowledgement strip

What you see A flowing strip directly below the top nav and above the first onboarding question. Cream-soft background, dark green left border, single paragraph plus a green Got it pill on the right. Non-blocking.
Verbatim
ETQ is a self-help planning tool, not financial advice. It does not assess your circumstances or recommend any course of action. Speak to a qualified professional before acting on a projection. Read the full disclaimer.
When First visit per browser. Suppressed by localStorage key etq:disclaimer:ack:v1. Re-shown after onboarding "Start over" or after clearing browser data.
How to trigger In DevTools console run:
localStorage.removeItem('etq:disclaimer:ack:v1') — then reload the onboarding page.

2.2 Nav Disclaimer link

What you see A small underlined "Disclaimer" link in the top nav, right of "Jump to full calculator →".
When Always visible.
How to trigger Look at the top of the onboarding page; the link is the rightmost item in the nav.

2.3 Persistent sticky bottom strip

What you see A thin strip pinned to the bottom of the viewport, centred. Cream-soft background, separated from page content by a top border.
Verbatim
Estimate, not advice. Result depends on the assumptions you choose. What's assumed.
When Always visible while the user is anywhere on the onboarding flow. Stays pinned as the user scrolls.
How to trigger Open onboarding and look at the bottom of the viewport. The strip is non-interactive everywhere except the "What's assumed" link, so clicks pass through to chips behind it.

2.4 Live in-progress estimate pip

What you see A small muted line beneath the live chart on the right-hand estimate panel.
Verbatim
Illustrative model output, not financial advice. See limitations.
When Visible as soon as the live-estimate panel mounts (which happens after the user has answered enough to produce an estimate).
How to trigger Answer the first few onboarding questions (age, take-home, living expenses, savings, investments) until the right-hand panel shows an age. The pip sits below the chart.

2.5 Handoff card (final onboarding screen)

What you see The final card the user reaches after completing the walkthrough. Two short paragraphs of disclosure copy. The last sentence is underlined.
Verbatim, paragraph 1
A starting estimate, not advice, based on your answers and ETQ's default assumptions for inflation, returns, pensions, and longevity. In the full calculator you can review and adjust all assumptions, plan for major events, schedule liquidations and more.
Verbatim, paragraph 2
Real-life outcomes will differ. Speak to a qualified professional before acting on the result.
When After the user has answered every active onboarding question. Reached automatically by completing the flow.
How to trigger Complete the onboarding walkthrough end-to-end (or open onboarding with a saved-but-completed state). The card sits at the bottom of the conversation list with a "Take me to the full calculator →" button.

2.6 Auto-restore toast

What you see A small floating notice in the bottom-right corner of the viewport. Cream background, accent left border, three elements: text + Forget link + close ×.
Verbatim
Restored your previous session from this browser. Forget ×
When On page load, when prior onboarding state is restored AND the user had answered at least one question. Auto-dismisses after 6 seconds.
How to trigger 1. Open onboarding. 2. Answer the first question (age) and press Enter. 3. Reload the page. The toast appears bottom-right.
"Forget" does Clears etq:scenario:draft:v2, etq:onboarding:state:v1, etq:landing:quick-estimate, etq:landing:inputs, etq:disclaimer:ack:v1, then reloads to a fresh start. Same effect as the nav "Start over" button.

Full calculator

URL: http://127.0.0.1:4173/ETQ/calculator.html

3.1 First-visit acknowledgement strip

What you see Same strip as §2.1, mounted above the calculator's three-panel layout.
When Same trigger flag (etq:disclaimer:ack:v1) shared with onboarding. Dismissing it on either surface suppresses it on both.
How to trigger In DevTools console: localStorage.removeItem('etq:disclaimer:ack:v1') then reload calculator.html.

3.2 Headline result pip

What you see A small subdued line directly below the "Earliest retirement [Now]" headline at the top of the dashboard.
Verbatim
Illustrative model output, not financial advice. Sensitive to your inputs and assumptions. See limitations.
When Always visible while the calculator is open.
How to trigger Open the calculator. The pip is at the top of the page, beneath the green "Earliest retirement" pill.

3.3 Headline message wording (now-case soften)

What you see The visible green pill says Now when the user can retire today. The wording change is in the underlying message string (used for accessibility and DOM semantics) — it now reads "With your assumptions, retirement looks viable now at X" instead of the earlier "You can retire now at X!". The pill text is unchanged because the pill is sized for short status words.
When When the calculator's current age equals the earliest viable retirement age (i.e., the user could retire today).
How to trigger In the calculator, set the current age, then enter inputs that yield a retirement age equal to the current age. Inspect the DOM via DevTools to see the message string in #retire-check-result; the user-visible word is still "Now".

3.4 Nav Disclaimer link

What you see A small underlined "Disclaimer" link in the dashboard header, on the right next to the "Enough to Quit" brand link.
When Always visible.
How to trigger Look at the top-right of the calculator page.

3.5 First-save inline notice

What you see A small note at the top of the scenario manager panel (left side of the calculator, "Current inputs" section). Cream background, accent left border, message + green Got it pill.
Verbatim
Saved on this device only. ETQ stores scenarios in your browser's local storage. Clearing browser data or switching device will lose them. There is no backup, no encryption, and no recovery; anyone using this browser profile can read these scenarios.
When The user has at least one saved scenario AND has not previously dismissed this notice. Suppressed by localStorage key etq:disclaimer:save-info-seen:v1.
How to trigger 1. Console: localStorage.removeItem('etq:disclaimer:save-info-seen:v1'). 2. In the scenario manager on the left, type a name (e.g. "Test") into the scenario name field. 3. Click Save. The notice appears above the input.

3.6 Auto-restore toast

What you see Same toast as §2.6, bottom-right.
When On calculator load, when a draft snapshot is auto-restored.
How to trigger 1. Open the calculator and enter some inputs (e.g. age, life expectancy, living expenses). 2. Reload the page. The toast appears.
"Forget" does Removes only the draft key etq:scenario:draft:v2 and reloads. Named scenarios saved in the scenario manager are preserved.

One static page reachable from every footer and nav. Five anchored sections.

4.1 Page-top interim notice

Verbatim
This page sets out ETQ's terms of use, the assumptions and limitations behind the tool, the risks you should consider before acting on a projection, and how data is handled. The wording is pending review by a Malta-qualified lawyer; the content is intended to be readable, accurate, and sufficient for everyday use of ETQ in the meantime.
When Always at the top of the page until lawyer-reviewed wording lands.

4.2 Anchor jumps and section content

Below the interim notice sits a row of in-page jump links. Five anchored sections follow, each with the legal-style content quoted in the privacy-legal-compliance reference document.

Reset cheatsheet

Use these from your browser's DevTools console to test any disclosure from a clean state.

Open DevTools

macOS Chrome / Edge / Brave: Cmd + Option + I. Click the Console tab. Paste the snippet, press Enter, then reload the page (Cmd + R).

Reset everything (every disclosure visible again) 
localStorage.clear(); sessionStorage.clear();
Then hard-reload (Cmd + Shift + R).
Re-show the first-visit acknowledgement strip (§2.1, §3.1) 
localStorage.removeItem('etq:disclaimer:ack:v1');
Re-show the first-save inline notice (§3.5) 
localStorage.removeItem('etq:disclaimer:save-info-seen:v1');
Then trigger a re-render of the scenario manager (e.g. click into and out of a field).
Re-trigger the auto-restore toast (§2.6, §3.6)

Onboarding: answer at least one question, then reload. Calculator: enter at least one input that gets auto-saved to the draft snapshot, then reload.

Inspect ETQ's storage

DevTools → Application tab → Storage → Local Storage / Session Storage → look for keys prefixed etq:. Five working keys plus two disclaimer keys: see the privacy-legal-compliance reference document for the full inventory.

Reading order tip. The fastest way to see every disclosure in one sitting is: 1) clear all storage, 2) open the landing page (covers §1.1-1.5), 3) click "Ease me in" to enter onboarding (covers §2.1-2.5), 4) on the handoff click "Take me to the full calculator" (covers §3.1-3.4), 5) save a scenario (covers §3.5), 6) reload the calculator (covers §3.6), 7) reload onboarding (covers §2.6), 8) click any "See limitations" link to reach the disclaimer page (covers §4).
